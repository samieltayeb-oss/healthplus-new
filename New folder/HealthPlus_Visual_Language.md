## 2. Core Photography Directives

Every future image for the Homepage, Services, About, Team, Contact, Appointment, and Clinic Interiors **must inherit** the following characteristics:

### 🏥 Architecture & Environment
- **Clean Modernism:** Crisp, white clinic walls. No clutter, no heavy decor.
- **Materiality:** Light, natural wood finishes that add organic warmth.
- **Subtle Branding:** Very subtle use of the HealthPlus Teal and Navy brand colors integrated naturally into the environment (e.g., subtle accents, upholstery, or trim).
- **Physical Signage:** If the HealthPlus logo appears, it must be fabricated as realistic, understated, three-dimensional architectural signage (matte acrylic or brushed metal) with physical depth (10-20mm) and a soft natural drop shadow. **Never** a flat graphic overlay.

### 💡 Lighting & Color Grading
- **Natural Daylight:** Bright, abundant natural light streaming through large windows.
- **Warmth:** A slightly warmed color temperature that feels healthy, inviting, and human. 
- **Shadows:** Soft, diffused natural shadows. No harsh or cinematic dramatic lighting.
- **Consistency:** The same exact color grading must be applied universally so the entire website looks like it was shot during a single cohesive photoshoot.

### 👥 Human Interaction
- **Diversity:** Genuine, diverse Canadian patients and professionals.
- **Subject Scale:** Subjects should be scaled appropriately (prominent focal points for heroes) to establish immediate emotional connection.
- **Expression:** Natural, warm smiles. Unhurried, compassionate, and professional interactions. 
- **Camera Awareness:** Subjects should **not** look directly at the camera.
- **Medical Context:** Discreet, realistic medical cues (a tablet, a clipboard, realistic equipment) to reinforce the clinical setting without being intimidating.

### 📷 Camera & Composition
- **Quality:** Ultra photorealistic, architectural magazine quality. 8K detail with realistic skin textures.
- **Depth of Field:** Architectural photography style. Use very subtle, softly blurred foreground elements (like the edge of a plant or desk) to add physical depth without distracting from the subject.
- **Copy-Safe Zones:** For all Hero imagery, generous negative space must be meticulously protected. The copy area must be completely free of prominent architectural elements or human subjects to allow typography and CTA buttons to overlay perfectly.
- **Background Life:** Incorporate minimal, softly blurred background activity (like a receptionist or distant patient) to give the clinic an operational, lived-in pulse without making it feel busy.

> [!WARNING]  
> **What to Avoid:**
> Do not use exaggerated stock-photo posing, floating objects, incorrect medical procedures, futuristic/holographic technology, dark cinematic lighting, heavy color grading, plastic skin textures, or fictional/AI-generated text within the environment.
