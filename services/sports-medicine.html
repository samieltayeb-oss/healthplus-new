Sports Medicine

Overview & Care Focus

Your sports medicine physician will conduct a comprehensive physical assessment of the injured area — evaluating range of motion, strength, stability, and pain. Based on the clinical findings, they may order imaging such as X-rays or MRI referrals to confirm the diagnosis. A detailed recovery timeline is created with you, and you will receive guidance on activity modification, physiotherapy coordination, bracing or taping, and a safe return-to-sport protocol. Our goal is to get you back to doing what you love — as quickly and safely as possible.

Who This Is For

Sports medicine is for anyone with an activity-related injury — from professional athletes and competitive team players to recreational fitness enthusiasts, gym-goers, weekend hikers, and individuals whose physical work has caused an injury. You do not need to be a competitive athlete to benefit from sports medicine care.

Preparation & What To Bring

Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that provides easy access to the injured area. Bring any previous imaging reports (X-rays, MRI, ultrasound) and a list of medications you are currently taking. If you have already been seen by another physician for this injury, bring a copy of their notes. Also think about your typical activity levels, training schedule, and the circumstances of the injury so your doctor can develop the most accurate plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to be a professional athlete to use this service? +
Not at all. We treat anyone with a sports or activity-related injury — from elite athletes to casual walkers, youth players, and adults returning to exercise after a break. If your pain or injury is limiting your movement or activity, sports medicine can help.
Do you offer physiotherapy on-site? +
Our physicians diagnose and design your recovery plan. We work closely with a trusted network of licensed physiotherapists in the community for your hands-on rehabilitation. We provide the referral and coordinate your care for a seamless recovery journey.
How long does recovery typically take? +
Recovery timelines vary widely depending on the type and severity of the injury. Minor sprains may resolve in 1–3 weeks with proper management, while ligament injuries or post-surgical recovery may take 3–6 months. Your physician will give you a realistic and personalized timeline at your first visit.
Can sports medicine doctors clear me for return to sport? +
Yes. A key role of sports medicine is formal return-to-sport clearance. We follow evidence-based return-to-play protocols that assess your strength, stability, and functional capacity before giving you the all-clear to resume full activity safely.

Conditions & Focus

  • Sprains, ligament tears, and joint instability
  • Muscle strains and tendon injuries (tendonitis, tendinopathy)
  • Stress fractures and overuse injuries
  • Shoulder, knee, hip, and ankle injuries
  • Return-to-sport planning after injury or surgery
  • Exercise-induced conditions and performance concerns
