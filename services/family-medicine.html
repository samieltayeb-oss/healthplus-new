Overview & Care Focus

Your family physician takes the time to understand your complete medical history, lifestyle, and current health concerns. At HealthPlus, we believe in building long-term, trusting relationships with our patients. During your visit you can expect a thorough physical exam, a review of your medications, preventive screening recommendations based on your age and risk factors, and an open conversation about any health concerns — from chronic disease management to mental wellness. We also coordinate with specialists and other healthcare providers to ensure your care is seamless and well-documented.

Who This Is For

Family medicine is for individuals and entire families of all ages who are looking for a consistent, trusted primary care provider. Whether you need routine check-ups, management of a long-term condition, immunizations for a child, or guidance navigating the healthcare system, our family physicians are your first and most important point of contact.

Preparation & What To Bring

For your first appointment, please bring your provincial health card (AHCIP), a complete list of all current medications and supplements, any previous medical records or lab results, and your personal or family medical history. If you are new to our clinic, arriving 10 minutes early allows time to complete any intake forms. We ask that you be as specific as possible about your concerns so we can make the most of your visit time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you accept new patients? +
Availability for new patients varies by physician. Please contact our clinic directly at (403) 254-4633 or visit in person to inquire about which family doctors are currently accepting new patients. We maintain a waitlist and do our best to accommodate everyone.
How often should I have a routine check-up? +
Adults aged 18–49 in good health are generally recommended to have a check-up every 2–3 years. Those 50 and older, or anyone with a chronic condition, typically benefit from annual visits. Your physician will advise you on the ideal frequency based on your personal health profile.
Can my family doctor manage my chronic condition? +
Absolutely. Family physicians are trained to manage most chronic conditions including hypertension, type 2 diabetes, hypothyroidism, asthma, and more. For conditions requiring specialist expertise, your family doctor will coordinate the appropriate referral.
Can I get a referral to a specialist through family medicine? +
Yes. Your family physician is your gateway to the specialist network. We coordinate referrals to internal medicine, cardiology, dermatology, orthopedics, and many other specialties based on your clinical needs.

Conditions & Focus

  • Routine annual check-ups and physicals
  • Chronic disease management (diabetes, hypertension, thyroid)
  • Preventive care and immunizations
  • Minor illnesses and respiratory infections
  • Referrals to specialists and diagnostic testing
  • Prescription management and renewals
