Overview & Care Focus

When you arrive at the walk-in clinic, our front desk team will register you and triage your concern. Patients are seen based on medical priority. A licensed physician will conduct a focused assessment, provide a diagnosis, and recommend a treatment plan — which may include prescriptions, requisitions for blood work or imaging, or a referral to a specialist or emergency care if required. Most visits take under 30 minutes from assessment to checkout.

Who This Is For

Our walk-in clinic is open to everyone — you do not need to be an existing patient. It is ideal for anyone experiencing an unexpected, non-life-threatening medical issue when their regular doctor is unavailable, or for those who do not yet have a family physician. If you are experiencing chest pain, difficulty breathing, or any signs of a medical emergency, please call 911 immediately.

Preparation & What To Bring

Simply bring your provincial health card. If you are on any medications, bring the bottles or a written list. If you are visiting for a work or school note, injury documentation, or a prescription renewal, let the front desk know so they can prepare the appropriate forms. No pre-registration or appointment is required.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an appointment? +
No appointment is necessary for our walk-in clinic. You can simply walk in during operating hours. However, patients with scheduled appointments will be seen first, so walk-in wait times may vary depending on clinic volume.
Can the walk-in doctor refill my prescription? +
Yes, in most cases our walk-in physicians can provide short-term prescription refills for ongoing medications, including blood pressure, thyroid, and allergy medications. Controlled substances and certain specialty medications may require your regular physician.
Will my walk-in visit be shared with my family doctor? +
Yes. With your consent, visit records are made available to your regular family physician to ensure continuity of care. This is especially important for ongoing conditions or follow-up needs.
What conditions are not suitable for the walk-in clinic? +
Chest pain, stroke symptoms, severe difficulty breathing, deep lacerations with heavy bleeding, or any life-threatening condition should be treated at the emergency department or by calling 911. The walk-in clinic is for non-emergency concerns only.

Conditions & Focus

  • Cold, flu, and respiratory infections
  • Minor cuts, lacerations, burns, or injuries
  • Rashes, skin irritations, and infections
  • Sprains, strains, and musculoskeletal pain
  • Ear infections, sore throats, and sinus pain
  • Urinary tract infections (UTIs)
  • Travel medicine and vaccination advice
